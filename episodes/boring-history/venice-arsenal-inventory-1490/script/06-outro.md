On the fifteenth of November, the day after the armoury is assessed, you compile the certification page. The certification page is the final leaf of the register, following the armoury section and preceding the rear binding. It summarises, in a fixed format prescribed by the Provveditori's office, the following items: the dates of the survey, the name of the scribe conducting it, the sections surveyed and the dates on which each was completed, the total number of items counted across all sections combined, the number of discrepancies noted, the number of outstanding queries generated by the survey and not yet transmitted, and the names of the witnesses who confirm the reliability of the count by co-signing the certification. The dates of the survey: fourth of November through fourteenth of November, fourteen hundred and ninety. The name of the scribe: your own, written in the formal hand used for signed documents. The sections surveyed: six, completed on the days as subsequently recorded. The total item count across all sections is, by this register's accounting, substantial. The cannon hall yielded one hundred and twelve ordnance pieces. The Tana produced one hundred and ninety-three registered rope coils, with a further forty unregistered and pending classification. The timber yard held seven hundred and seventy-nine planks in various stages of seasoning. The galley sheds contained thirty-eight hulls in three condition grades. The oar stores accounted for twenty-two thousand four hundred and eleven registered oars, including those on loan. The anchor inventory covered sixty pieces, including the Bucintoro's two. The armoury rounded out the count with three thousand two hundred and fourteen serviceable items of arms and armour. The number of discrepancies, which is to say the number of individual locations where the count or condition differs from the spring register, is forty-seven, of which thirty-one are minor and attributable to counting imprecision, twelve represent genuine changes in condition or quantity, and four are of a kind that cannot be resolved at the level of the survey and that require Provveditori action. The four are: the missing falconetto number fourteen, the 1487 petriera bore discrepancy, the Famagusta oar loan, and the condemned hull accounting treatment. The certification page is signed by you and by Pietro Zane, who was present on the final day, and by Zuan Battista, the shed foreman, who comes to the scribes' room in the afternoon for this purpose. Nicolò Falier, the proto da marangon, had been asked to co-sign and sends word that he will come in the morning; he comes in the morning, signs, and leaves. The certification page is complete on the sixteenth of November.

The register is transmitted to the Provveditori all'Arsenal on the seventeenth of November, carried by the Arsenal's own internal courier from the scribes' room to the Provveditori's office, a distance within the Arsenal complex of perhaps two hundred and fifty paces. A receipt of transmission is made, signed by the Provveditori's clerk, and a copy is left in the scribes' room file. You are not present when the register is received by the Provveditori; your assignment ends with the transmission. In December of fourteen ninety, the Provveditori issues three written queries arising from the winter register. The first query concerns the missing falconetto, number fourteen: the Provveditori requests that the Arsenal's guard register be searched for any transfer record, and that a physical search of the Nuovo section be conducted, particularly the area near the rigging stores where pieces have occasionally been moved during maintenance work. The second query concerns the bore measurement discrepancy of the petriera grossa: the Provveditori requests that the ordnance master open the relevant section of the winter register of fourteen eighty-seven and compare the gauge specifications used in that year with those currently in use, to determine whether a calibre-gauge difference might explain the readings. The third query concerns the Famagusta oar loan: the Provveditori notes that the Collegio query submitted by the scribe Bartolomeo Sanudo in March fourteen ninety has not produced a ruling, and instructs the Arsenal's administrative office to re-submit the same query with a notation as to the time elapsed since the first submission. Whatever responses to these three queries were written, they have not been located by researchers working from the same archival source as this register. The query regarding the condemned hull accounting treatment — the fourth outstanding matter noted in the certification page — is not addressed in any of the three December queries from the Provveditori, because it was already the subject of the inquiry initiated in fourteen eighty-eight, which was still open, and the Provveditori's office treated it as already in process. Whether that inquiry was ever formally concluded is not documented in the holdings associated with this register. The winter inventory register of fourteen ninety survives in the Archivio di Stato di Venezia, in the Provveditori all'Arsenal fond, catalogue reference Provveditori all'Arsenal, busta seventeen, fascicolo four. It is one of approximately two hundred and thirty inventory registers from the period fourteen sixty through fifteen ten held in that collection. The certification page bears four signatures. That is all that remains.

The Provveditori's three queries, received on the twenty-second of December, require written responses within thirty days of receipt, which sets a deadline of the twenty-first of January fourteen ninety-one. The first query — locate the missing falconetto bearing catalogue number fourteen — is forwarded to the proto delle artiglierie with a note requesting a search of the foundry repair records and the outgoing issue records for the year fourteen ninety. The second query — compare the calibre gauge records from the fourteen eighty-seven survey against the current measurements for the petriera grossa — requires the search of the foundry archives for any notation of bore repair or re-lining performed on item number forty-one of the petriera class between fourteen eighty-seven and the present. You draft the letter requesting this search on the twenty-fourth of December and submit it to the Arsenal's administrative office. The third query — re-submit the Cyprus oar question to the Collegio — is addressed by copying the letter of March fourteen ninety, the one submitted by Bartolomeo Sanudo and noted in the spring register as unanswered, and transmitting a new copy with a covering note indicating that the matter has now been outstanding for nine months and that the winter register of fourteen ninety confirms the oars have not returned and no compensation has been received. This new submission is prepared on the twenty-sixth of December and dispatched through the Arsenal's messenger service to the Collegio on the second of January fourteen ninety-one. Whether the Collegio will respond to the second letter is not known at the time of drafting. The condemned hull inquiry, as noted, has been open since fourteen eighty-eight and is not re-queried; the query is already, by the Provveditori's own assessment, before the Senate, and a further letter from the scribes' room would be duplicative and potentially viewed as impertinent. The winter register of fourteen ninety is complete. It is transmitted to the Provveditori on the seventeenth of November. It survives.


The register contains one hundred and four written pages in the scribes' room's standard cahier format, with entries written in the hand of a single scribe across all thirty-five days of production — the eleven survey days plus the days of consolidation and drafting that follow. The binding is the same as the spring register binding: a leather board cover, two cord ties at the fore-edge, the Arsenal's seal stamped on the inside cover. The paper is locally produced. The ink has not faded. The handwriting is consistent and remains legible in the copy deposited at the Archivio di Stato three years after the survey, when the Provveditori's office undertook a general transfer of administrative documents. The three follow-up queries produced responses of varying completeness. The foundry search for the missing falconetto found no dispatch record. The calibre gauge comparison for the petriera grossa found a notation, dated fourteen eighty-nine, of a bore lining operation performed on item forty-one of the petriera class, confirming the measurement discrepancy as a result of an undocumented repair. The Cyprus oar query, as previously noted, received no reply from the Collegio. That is all.
